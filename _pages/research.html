We integrate experiments, theory, computational modeling, AI and AM to understand extreme events and extreme conditions. Below are our key research areas.

Project 1: Hypervelocity Impact on Competent Rocks

This research aims to understand the behavior of brittle rocks subjected to hypervelocity impacts. We use the HyFIRE facility in HEMI at Johns Hopkins University to impact a basalt cube at 5.0 km/s with a 3 mm, aluminum sphere. In-situ diagnostics including ultrahigh speed camerates at 2 millions fps and flash X-ray are integrated to visualize the impact process. In these experiments, we are particularly interested in measuring the impact crater diameter and ejecta angle as a function of time. Our experimental results are useful for planetary science and defense communities to benchmark hydrocodes and validate constitutive models.
